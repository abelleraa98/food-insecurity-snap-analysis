# Food Insecurity and SNAP Access in the United States: A Data Analysis

---

## Project Overview

Food insecurity remains a persistent issue in the United States, affecting millions of households each year. Government assistance programs such as the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) are designed to reduce food insecurity by providing financial support for food access.

This project analyzes the relationship between food insecurity, poverty, and SNAP participation in the United States. By comparing measures of need (poverty and food insecurity) with measures of assistance (SNAP participation and benefits), the analysis aims to identify potential gaps between those who need support and those who receive it. 

---

## Research Question

How do poverty and food insecurity compare to SNAP participants in the United States?

---

## Secondary Questions

1. How does food insecurity vary across states?
2. How does poverty relate to food insecurity?
3. Do higher levels of SNAP participation correspond with lower food insecurity?
4. Are there differences across states in the relationship between need and assistance?

## Analysis Plan

This project examines the relationship between need and assistance through four key analyses:

1. **Food Insecurity by State**

    Analyze how food insecurity varies across states.

2. **Poverty and Food Insecurity**

   Examine the relationship between poverty rates and food insecurity rates.

3. **SNAP Participation and Food Insecurity**

   Compare SNAP participation levels with insecurity rates.

4. **SNAP Benefits and Food Insecurity**

   Explore whether higher SNAP benefits correspond with lower food insecurity.
